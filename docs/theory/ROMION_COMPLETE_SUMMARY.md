## 2. Fundamental Ontology: CORE and FRACTURE

The basic distinction divides reality into two layers:

### CORE

* Pre-emergent relational layer
* Has no space, time, or matter
* Is a **hypergraph of relations without edge/boundary**
* Relations tend toward entangling and minimizing pressure cost
* No global time or global ordering exists in it

### FRACTURE

* Emergent layer
* Arises through **stabilization of cheap relational loops**
* Locally generates:
  * time
  * space
  * objects
  * laws of physics as stable schemas
* Is the only layer accessible to direct observation

### CORE/FRACTURE Boundary

* Thin, highly active layer
* **Actual engine of emergence**
* Where:
  * relations negotiate stability
  * "noise" appears
  * projection of relations onto metric occurs

## 3. Edgeless Hypergraph — Consequences

The relational hypergraph **cannot have an edge** because:

* An edge would imply:
  * end
  * beginning
  * possibility of global cycle
* This would introduce:
  * finiteness
  * global time
  * possibility of cyclic universe overlay

Consequences:

* Universe **is not finite**
* **Is not cyclic**
* **Does not repeat globally**
* **Local emergence domains** can arise in different regions of hypergraph
* But there is no "next Universe after this one"

This is NOT multiverse:

* There is no collection of separate universes
* There are local emergence phases in one boundaryless relational structure

## 4. Time and "Age of Universe"

### Key Distinction:

ROMION O'LOGIC **rejects the concept of absolute age of Universe**.

* Time **did not exist before emergence**
* One cannot speak of "how many years were before"
* Any attempt to rewind seconds is a category error

Therefore introduced is the concept:

> **"observable impression of Universe age"**
> or
> **"illusion of Universe age in conditions of present observability of its evolution"**

This impression:

* Concerns only the FRACTURE phase
* Results from current description tools
* **Can exceed 16 billion years** without contradiction with data
* Is not an ontological property of Universe, but a description parameter

Key statement:

> **Universe has no age – it only has observable impression of time since it became measurable.**
