## 1. De Sitter School: Methodological Completion

### What Was Correct (Preserved)

The de Sitter school provided:
1. **Coherent metric formalism** - geometry of expansion as field equation solution
2. **Observability horizon** as geometric consequence
3. **Useful approximation** - "in certain regime, Universe behaves de Sitter-like"

These are conclusions about **how projection onto metric looks** - and this is OK.

### What Was Missing: Separation of Description from Ontology

The error was not mathematical but interpretational:

**Description:** "Metric behaves as if Λ component exists"

**Unwarranted transition:** "Therefore vacuum energy / substance / fundamental constant exists"

ROMION introduces the missing methodological step:

> That something works like Λ in equations doesn't mean Λ is an entity.
> It only means there's a process whose projection has that form.

### Corrected Methodology

| Old Question | New Question |
|--------------|--------------|
| "What is Λ?" | "What relational conditions generate Λ-type projection?" |

This shifts discussion from metaphysics ("what substance?") to methodology ("what system state gives this description?").

### De Sitter in ROMION Terms

De Sitter is not "vacuum with vacuum energy" but:

> A state where relational pressure balance (CORE) dominates over the cost of maintaining and reconfiguring emergent structures (FRACTURE).

Meaning:
- Λ is not "property of vacuum" as substance
- Λ is effective parameter describing CORE relational pressure projected onto emergent metric

**Ontological translation:**
```
de Sitter = metric shadow of situation where:
    - FRACTURE is relatively "frozen" (repetitive schemas, little new emergence)
    - CORE still generates relational growth (no edge = no stop)
    - Projection onto geometry looks like constant acceleration
```

### Why De Sitter is "Attractor"

In many frameworks, cosmology with Λ tends toward de Sitter in far future.

Romionologically this is natural:
- The more FRACTURE "stiffens", less pressure goes to structure reconstruction
- What remains is purer CORE dominance → metric approaches de Sitter

**De Sitter is state of minimal emergence dynamics complexity with continuous relational growth in CORE.**

### Anchor Statements

> "de Sitter w romionologii nie jest właściwością próżni, lecz geometryczną projekcją dominacji presji relacyjnej CORE nad kosztem stabilizacji FRACTURE."

> "Λ to nie byt – to bilans."

> "de Sitter jest prawidłowym przybliżeniem, ale błędnym fundamentem."

## 2. Friedmann School: What He Unconsciously Described

### What Friedmann Said (Standard Interpretation)

In Friedmann equations:
- Expansion rate depends on "density" (ρ)
- Critical density (ρ_c) separates geometry behavior regimes
- Curvature (k) and expansion fate are linked to this balance

This is formally correct at metric level.

### Romionological Translation

**Friedmann (using purely metric method) described not "matter ruling space" but — in romionological reading — the balance of emergence stabilization versus background pressure, only he lacked language to name it.**

### Point-by-Point Translation

#### "Universe Density" = Not Amount of Substance

In ROMION, ρ is not "how much matter is in space" but **how much relational budget is bound in stable loops (FRACTURE)**:

- High ρ ⇒ many relations "frozen" in lasting schemas (objects, fields, masses)
- Low ρ ⇒ relations less bound in structure, more "flowing" in CORE-pressure

**Friedmann unconsciously described:**
> How strongly emergence (FRACTURE) draws relational budget and thereby affects metric.

#### "Gravitational Braking of Expansion" = Cost of Structure Stabilization

In his language: mass/density brakes expansion.

In romionological: the more stable loops, the greater pressure cost of maintaining geometry and the stronger metric "pulling".

**This is not "matter as weight" but:**
> FRACTURE as load on relational balance at CORE/FRACTURE boundary.

#### Critical Density ρ_c = Phase Threshold, Not Magic Number

ρ_c in ROMION is equivalent to:

> "How much FRACTURE is needed for metric response to dominate"
> vs
> "When CORE-pressure dominates and metric escapes"

**ρ_c is boundary of regimes:**
- FRACTURE-domination (strong closure, strong gravity)
- CORE-domination (easier expansion projection)

**Friedmann described phase/transition condition without knowledge of CORE.**

#### Curvature k = Emergent Information About Closure Topology

In standard: k is "geometry of space".

In ROMION: k is more like:
> Signature of how relational hypergraph locally closes loops and what effective "metric shortcuts/constraints" it gives in FRACTURE.

This explains why k is globally difficult parameter: **because it's projection, not "shape of thing itself".**

### What Friedmann Unconsciously Described

> "Friedmann nieświadomie opisał bilans pomiędzy presją relacyjną tła (CORE) a kosztem stabilizacji struktur (FRACTURE), który w projekcji metrycznej wygląda jak zależność ekspansji od gęstości i próg gęstości krytycznej."

### Reinterpretation of "Density's Effect on Universe"

| Classical View | ROMION View |
|----------------|-------------|
| "Density" = how much substance in space | "Density" = how frozen is world in repeatable loops |
| High density = lots of matter | High density = strong FRACTURE dominance (expansion braking as stabilization cost) |
| Low density = little matter | Low density = CORE dominance (easier expansion projection) |
