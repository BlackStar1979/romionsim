# SPEC — Loop Classes in ROMION

## Role

Structural specification describing **classes of Δ‑loop states**
used to organize and interpret emergent matter‑like structures in ROMION.

This document:
- introduces **classifications**, not new entities,
- groups loop states by structural similarity,
- prepares ground for later domain mappings.

Ontological authority remains exclusively in THEORY_V3.9.md.

Category: SPEC

## 1. Purpose of Loop Classification

Δ‑loops exist ontologically as defined in THEORY.
However, for analysis and interpretation
it is useful to group loop states into **classes**.

Such classes:
- do not alter existence rules,
- do not introduce new primitives,
- are organizational tools.

They support:
- comparison of configurations,
- identification of stable composites,
- later mapping to external physics domains.

---

## 2. Classification Principles

Loop classes are defined using combinations of:
- topological length L₀,
- stability μ(C),
- orientation σ(C),
- topological charge Q_T,
- niche parameters.

A classification:
- must be explicit,
- must be reproducible,
- must not override the Topological Exclusion Principle.

---

## 3. Primary Loop Classes

### 3.1 Quark‑Like Loops

Quark‑like loops are characterized by:
- small to moderate topological length,
- relatively high stability μ,
- strong localization within a single niche,
- participation in composite bundles.

They are:
- not particles,
- not point objects,
- elementary resonant structures in the relational fabric.

---

### 3.2 Lepton‑Like Loops

Lepton‑like loops are characterized by:
- absence of degeneracy under loop symmetries,
- relative isolation from composite binding,
- stable persistence without bundling.

They represent:
- individually stable resonances,
- minimal structural complexity within FRACTURE.

---

### 3.3 Composite Loops

Composite loops emerge from:
- fused loop structures,
- nested configurations,
- high overlap bundles.

Composite loops may exhibit:
- increased stability,
- extended topological reach,
- multi‑niche influence.

Composite does not imply fundamental.

---

## 4. Bundle‑Based Classes

### 4.1 Baryon‑Like Bundles

A baryon‑like structure is defined as:
- a small set of compatible loops,
- bound within the same core cluster,
- exhibiting collective stability exceeding individual components.

Such bundles:
- require algebraic operations (see SPEC_LOOP_ALGEBRA.md),
- are emergent composites, not primitives.

---

### 4.2 Meson‑Like Pairs

Meson‑like structures consist of:
- paired loops,
- high mutual overlap,
- complementary orientation or charge.

They are transient or semi‑stable
depending on environmental conditions.

---

## 5. Degeneracy and Internal Labels

Some loop classes may admit **degeneracy**:
- multiple loop states occupy related structural roles,
- but are distinguished by internal labels.

Degeneracy has no ontological meaning by itself.
It becomes relevant only in:
- composite stability,
- exclusion constraints,
- domain mappings.

---

## 6. Relationship to Exclusion

All loop classes remain subject to:
- the Topological Exclusion Principle,
- niche uniqueness constraints.

Classification does not relax exclusion rules.
