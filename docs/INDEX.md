# ROMION Documentation Index

## Role

Master index and epistemic map of the ROMION project documentation.

This document defines:
- which files are canonical,
- which are explanatory, structural, interpretative, or experimental,
- and how all documents relate to the canonical ontology.

This is a governance and navigation document.
It does not define ontology, mechanics, or physical claims.

---

## Epistemic Categories

Each document belongs to exactly one category,
defined by its filename and declared role.

---

### THEORY (Canonical Ontology)

Defines what exists and the rules of existence.
There is exactly one canonical theory document.

---

### FOUNDATION (Formal Bases)

Formal explanation of assumptions used in THEORY.
No new entities or dynamics may be introduced.

---

### MECHANICS (Deductive)

Derives emergent behavior from canonical ontology.
No new ontological commitments.

---

### SPEC (Structural Extensions)

Structural and operational extensions.
May evolve independently of THEORY.

---

### POTENTIALS (Orientational)

Catalog of explanatory and predictive possibilities.
No claims, no hypotheses, no ontology.

---

### MAP (Domain Mappings)

Interpretative mappings to physical and cosmological language.
Explicitly non-canonical.

---

### HYPOTHESIS (Testable Claims)

Explicit, falsifiable hypotheses.
Never ontological.

---

### GLOSSARY (Terminology)

Language alignment only.
No ontological authority.

## Governance Rules

1. Ontology exists only in THEORY_V3.9.md.
2. No document may redefine existence.
3. Interpretation and explanation are separate.
4. POTENTIALS list possibilities only.
5. Hypotheses must be falsifiable.
6. Domain language is confined to MAP,
   HYPOTHESIS, and POTENTIALS.
