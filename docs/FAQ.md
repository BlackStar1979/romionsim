# FAQ — ROMION O'LOGIC

This document answers frequently asked questions
about the ROMION O'LOGIC project.

It is intended for readers who want a clear orientation
without reading the full documentation set.

This document explains usage and intent,
not ontology.

---

## 1. What is ROMION O'LOGIC?

ROMION O'LOGIC is a relational ontology and epistemic framework.

It focuses on:
- what exists at the most fundamental level,
- how observable structure can emerge,
- how explanations should be separated from ontology.

It does not attempt to replace existing physical theories.

---

## 2. Is ROMION a physical theory?

No.

ROMION is not a competing physical theory
and does not propose alternative equations
to quantum mechanics, general relativity, or cosmology.

It is an ontological and methodological framework
that clarifies what existing theories describe
and what they implicitly assume.

---

## 3. What does ROMION assume exists?

At the canonical level, ROMION assumes only relations.

There are:
- no fundamental objects,
- no fundamental particles,
- no fundamental spacetime.

Observable entities arise only after emergence
and stabilization.

---

## 4. What are CORE and FRACTURE?

CORE is the pre-emergent relational phase.
It has:
- no space,
- no time,
- no objects.

FRACTURE is the emergent phase.
It is where:
- space and time appear,
- stable structures form,
- observation becomes possible.

Emergence occurs at a boundary between these phases.

---

## 5. Does ROMION deny particles or fields?

ROMION does not deny observed particles or fields.

It states that they are not fundamental entities.
They are interpreted as stable relational configurations
or modes of interaction at the emergent level.

Particle language is used only in interpretative documents.

---

## 6. Does ROMION explain quantum mechanics?

ROMION does not replace quantum mechanics
and does not modify its mathematical formalism.

It provides an ontological perspective
that can reinterpret quantum phenomena
such as superposition, entanglement, and measurement
without introducing additional postulates.

These interpretations remain non-canonical.

---

## 7. Does ROMION explain gravity or cosmology?

ROMION does not replace general relativity or cosmology.

It offers ways in which:
- gravitational effects,
- cosmological parameters,
- large-scale structure

may be interpreted as emergent consequences
of relational structure.

All such explanations are explicitly non-canonical.

---

## 8. What is the purpose of POTENTIALS.md?

POTENTIALS.md is an orientational document.

It collects:
- possible explanations,
- possible interpretations,
- possible predictions

enabled by ROMION,
without asserting that any of them are correct.

It is a catalog of possibilities, not claims.

---

## 9. What is the difference between MAP and HYPOTHESIS documents?

MAP documents provide interpretative mappings
from ROMION concepts to physics and cosmology language.

HYPOTHESIS documents contain testable claims
with explicit alternatives and falsification criteria.

MAP explains descriptions.
HYPOTHESIS tests possibilities.

Neither defines ontology.

---

## 10. Can ROMION be tested experimentally?

ROMION itself is an ontology and is not directly testable.

However, some ideas derived from it
can be formulated as explicit hypotheses
and tested in simulations or experiments.

Such tests are documented only in HYPOTHESIS files.

---

## 11. Why is the documentation so strict?

ROMION enforces strict separation
between existence, explanation, and interpretation.

This prevents:
- smuggling assumptions into ontology,
- treating metaphors as explanations,
- confusing descriptions with reality.

The structure exists to maintain clarity at scale.

---

## 12. Who is ROMION for?

ROMION is for:
- researchers interested in foundations,
- people working at the boundary of physics and ontology,
- those concerned with interpretative consistency.

It is not designed as a teaching textbook
or a ready-made physical model.

---

## 13. Is ROMION finished?

No.

The ontology is stable at the current version.
Interpretations and hypotheses are open.

ROMION is designed to remain minimal at its core
while allowing controlled exploration at the edges.

ROMION makes no promises of ultimate explanation.

Its goal is more modest:
to ensure that when explanations are offered,
their status is explicit and disciplined.
