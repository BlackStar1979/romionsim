## 2. Relational Pressure and Cost

At the CORE level, relational configurations evolve toward
lower contradiction / lower relational cost.

This tendency does not encode:
- time evolution,
- physical force,
- or causal direction.

Instead, it expresses a **local preference** for configurations
with reduced negotiation tension.

This manifests operationally as:
- pressure gradients,
- reconfiguration frequency differences,
- stabilization bias.

→ Ontology: THEORY_V3.9.md, Section II.1

## 3. Boundary‑Driven Emergence

Emergence occurs at the CORE / FRACTURE boundary.

Key properties:
- generation of candidate relational structures,
- selection via stabilization cost,
- rejection of unstable paths.

Emergence is therefore:
- active,
- selective,
- competitive.

It is not a threshold crossing or instantaneous projection.

Δ‑loops arise when:

## 5. Pressure Gradients and Flow

Regions with:
- many stabilized loops,
- high relational density,

exhibit:
- increased local cost of reconfiguration,
- reduced relational mobility.

Conversely, sparse regions:
- support easier reconfiguration,
- permit long relational shortcuts.

These gradients appear observationally as:
- attraction,
- resistance,
- directional flow.

No “force” is invoked at the ontology level.

Relations surviving the w_bridge threshold

- dynamic relational channels,

## 7. Freeze and Thaw Dynamics

A configuration enters a **freeze state** when:
- no bridge relations remain active,
- interaction weight drops below observability.

During freeze:
- structure persists,
- but no propagation occurs.

Thawing occurs when:
- new bridges stabilize,
- boundary activity increases,
- interaction channels reopen.

Freeze is thus a **phase behavior**,
not a termination or failure.

→ Ontology: THEORY_V3.9.md, Section II.4

without violating relational locality.

No signal travels faster than allowed structure permits;

Time in ROMION:

Low pressure regions:
- evolve slowly,
- support long‑lived structures.

Time rate is therefore:
**a function of relational pressure**, not a global parameter.
