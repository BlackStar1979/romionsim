## 1. Relational Hypergraph Assumption

ROMION assumes that reality at the CORE level is structured as a
**relational hypergraph**:

- nodes represent relational units (romions),
- hyperedges represent collective relations among ≥2 nodes,
- no embedding in space or time is assumed.

This assumption is ontological and precedes all projection or observation.

→ Ontology: THEORY_V3.9.md, Section I

---

## 2. The Edgeless (No-Boundary) Principle

The relational hypergraph has **no global boundary**.

A boundary would imply at least one of:
- an absolute beginning or end,
- a globally privileged reference location,
- the possibility of a global cycle.

Any of these would introduce:
- finiteness,
- global ordering,
- or global time.

All are explicitly excluded by the canonical ontology.

→ Ontology: THEORY_V3.9.md, Section I.2

---

## 3. Consequences of Edgelessness

From the no-boundary principle follow immediately:

### 3.1 No Global Time
- No universal clock exists.
- Ordering can arise only locally after stabilization.

### 3.2 No Global Cycles
- The hypergraph cannot return to a previous global state.
- Local loops are allowed; global recurrence is not.

### 3.3 No Absolute Origin
- Questions of “before” emergence are ill-posed.
- There is no meaningful global age of reality.

These are **ontological consequences**, not explanatory hypotheses.

---

## 4. Relational Infinity

ROMION defines infinity as **relational non-closure**, not spatial extension.

This means:
- there is no finite description covering all relations,
- there is no complete global map of the hypergraph,
- any representation is necessarily local and partial.

This does **not** imply:
- infinite spatial extent,
- infinite number of nodes at any given time,
- or infinite observable content.

→ Ontology: THEORY_V3.9.md, Section I.3

---

## 5. Locality Without Metric

At the CORE level:
- locality is defined purely by **relational adjacency**,
- metric notions (distance, length) do not exist.

Two romions are CORE-local if:
- they participate in a common hyperrelation,
- or are connected by a continuous relational chain.

Metric distance appears only after projection into FRACTURE
and must not be retrojected into CORE.

---

## 6. Path Potential and Apparent Dimensionality

The relational hypergraph supports a potentially very large number of
distinct relational paths between nodes.

Consequences:
- multiple effective degrees of freedom may appear,
- dense path availability may be misinterpreted as extra dimensions.

ROMION rejects:
- additional spatial dimensions,
- hidden geometric axes.

The appearance of high-dimensional behavior is a **path-combinatorial effect**,
not a geometric one.

→ Ontology: THEORY_V3.9.md, Section I.4

---

## 7. Compatibility With Finite Representations

Any simulation or model uses finite graphs for technical reasons.

Such representations:
- approximate an edgeless hypergraph locally,
- must be monitored for boundary artifacts.

Boundary effects in simulations are:
- implementation artifacts,
- not predictions of the theory.

This constraint applies to all numerical work.
